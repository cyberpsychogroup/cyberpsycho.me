Skills & interests

The group has a strong interest in psychometrics as well as using open source software, R, to develop papers and workshops.

This way we can contribute to the community by developing reproducible research and accumulate resources for other researchers.

Our particular interests are in the following areas:

Item Response Theory

Item Response Theory (IRT) is an approach in psychometrics that is used to design, analyse and evaluate of tests, questionnaires, and similar instruments. It has wide applicability and is usually found in any studies measuring abilities, attitudes, or other variables.

This approach is based on the assumption that there is a relationship between participant’s performances on a test item and the participant’s levels of performance on an overall measure of the ability that the test item was designed to measure.

The key difference from a more classic approach is that IRT does not assume that all items are equally difficult. To incorporate scaling items IRT uses the difficulty of test items.

Exploratory and Confirmatory procedures:

Our particular interest is on using Exploratory Structural Equation Modeling (ESEM): development of the reproducible research and packages as well as application of the EFA in cyberpsychology.

ESEM is a relatively new technique that leverages the advantages of both Exploratory/Confirmatory Factor Analysis (EFA/CFA) and Structural Equation Modeling (SEM).

Within other techniques and approaches, we are doing work in

  • Measurement Invariance

  • Multilevel Analysis

  • Cross-Lagged Designs

Network Analysis: we live in the Net, and not only in the Web-sense

Network analysis (or Social Network analysis, SNA) is the approach to investigating social structures by using networks and graph theory. This approach reviews each phenomenon or event as a set of nodes and and links between them (i.e. ties or edges).

Our particular focus is on applying network analysis to social media data where we develop applications and packages to collect, classify, categorise social media posts and users.

But SNA has much more to offer! It can be used to examine patterns of relationships between causal factors to identify the focal event and develop a model of the more accurate perceived causal structure.

Machine Learning and Data Mining

Machine learning is a set of methods that is used to detect patterns in data to predict future events or occurrences. It has numerous applications, including psychology and mental health. There are quite a few methods in this domain with some examples of applications, including classifying medical cases, identifying groups of online gamers, detecting patterns in online behaviour, predicting anxiety and depression, determining interactive factors that influence mental conditions.
