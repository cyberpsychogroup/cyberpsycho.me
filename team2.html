Vas

Team Leader

Dr Vasileios Stavropoulos is an internationally reputable academic and clinician in the broader field of applied psychology, with an emphasis on the use-abuse of digital media and online games. His work has been acknowledged and furthered by: a) receiving the Australian Research Council Discovery Early Career Researcher Award, 2021 and; b) membership in the specialized task force for the revision of Internet Gaming Disorder as a formal diagnosis in the Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders (DSM-5). Vasileios is a member of the Australian Psychological Society (APS), the APS Clinical College, a registered psychologist endorsed in Clinical Psychology by the Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Authority (AHPRA), and an accredited supervisor with the same institution. Vasileios currently works as a Senior Research Fellow in the Institute for Health and Sport, Victoria University, where he leads the Cyberpsychology and Online behavior research team.

As a scientist-practitioner, Vasileios’ research questions stem from his clinical experience and adhere to the principles of person-focused developmental research. His theoretical conceptualization follows the differential susceptibility and the risk and resilience frameworks. Within this context, Vasileios’ most recent research interests move in the field of digital phenotyping for mental health. Therefore, he aims to translate one’s digital media and gaming usage patterns into information about their mental health conditions, while combining multi-method data such as psychometric scales, wearable actigraphy, mobile monitoring applications, and social media posts. He examines this data by emphasizing the longitudinal interplay between individual, contextual and digital factors that result in the development of behavior. Vasileios’ versatile modelling and psychometric skills enable him to operationalize his research questions via a range of analyses, such as item response theory, invariance, growth, mixture, and network modelling, through the use of different programs including R, HLM, M-plus, IRTpro, JASP and Jamovi.

Maria

Data guru & tech wonder girl

Dr Maria Prokofieva is a senior lecturer in the School of Business at Victoria University (Australia). She loves data and number crunching. She works in data science and is interested in applying data science to real world problems. Her interest is at the intersection of business and psychology.

A particular passion is on digital media (aka social media) and research methods.

Business communication, digital media, what drives people’s behaviour and how to analyse it with data – just a few topics to list!

Maria is passionate about using open source – R and promotes its use through development of workshops and educational courses. She runs a RBusiness development group from R Consortium as well as she runs a CPA Australia Discussion group on Business analytics.

Daniel

Daniel Zarate is a Ph.D. candidate in clinical psychology with an emphasis on online behavioral addictions. He currently works in research-related and teaching roles, mostly teaching in psychology, counselling, and research methodologies in undergraduate and postgraduate programs. his main research interests are in the areas of clinical psychology, behavioral addictions, and complex statistical models. Additionally, his Ph.D. uses Natural Language processing to focus on decoding ‘digital phenotypes’ in mental health contexts to detect and predict psychological issues such as anxiety and depression. These projects have enabled him to develop and implement programming skills in R Studio and state-of-the-art methodologies.

Taylor

Taylor Brown is a PhD candidate at Victoria University and a registered provisional psychologist with the Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Authority (AHPRA). Taylor’s research interests include areas of (Tele)Presence, Behavioural Addictions, Psychopathology, and Psychometry. Her research explores the connection one has with their avatar and the game world, and how this might provide some information about how and who they are outside of the game. Taylor’s interest in video games stemmed from her interest as a young child, particularly in creating different worlds through Sims. This led Taylor to pursue her interest in gaming through her research.

Deon

Deon Tullett-Prado is a PhD candidate and emerging researcher in the area of behavioural addictions and in particular Digital Addictions. His current work features investigating the line between ‘normal’ of even ‘adaptive’ social media use and ‘problematic’ or ‘Maladaptive’ Social media use - making use of advanced statistical techniques such as Latent Class Analysis, Network Analysis and Growth Mixture Modelling. Expertise includes the use of innovative and advanced statistical analyses, and in particular methods that involve profiling populations or identifying patterns of interaction in networks.
